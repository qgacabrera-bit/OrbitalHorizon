Orbital Horizon

The Limit to Vision.

About

Orbital Horizon is an interactive website created to investigate exoplanetary findings through actual NASA records. Based on the Kepler, K2 and TESS missions, the project turns raw astronomical data into insights that are easily accessible so that users can understand how scientists detect the presence of potential planets beyond our solar system.

Our Mission

We created Orbital Horizon to bridge the gap between advanced astrophysical data and curious minds. The Kepler, K2, and TESS missions have generated vast amounts of valuable data, but much of it remains highly technical. Our goal is to make these groundbreaking discoveries approachable for students, researchers, and space enthusiasts alike. By combining machine learning with interactive visualizations, we aim to inspire curiosity and foster a deeper understanding of exoplanet exploration.

How It Works

Orbital Horizon is built on meticulously curated exoplanet data from NASA’s Kepler, K2, and TESS missions, encompassing thousands of potential exoplanets. We clean and preprocess this data to ensure accuracy, focusing on key attributes like orbital period, stellar radius, transit depth, and host star properties. Through Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA), we uncover meaningful patterns and relationships within the data.

Using advanced machine learning, we trained classification models to distinguish between confirmed planets, false positives, and potential exoplanets. The most effective model powers our platform’s predictive capabilities. Paired with immersive 3D visualizations created using Three.js and custom tools, Orbital Horizon offers real-time, interactive simulations of planetary systems, bringing the science of exoplanet detection to life.

What We Offer

Orbital Horizon combines cutting-edge predictive modeling with captivating visualizations. Our machine learning model calculates the likelihood of a celestial object being an exoplanet, while our visualization engine renders dynamic, real-time star systems. By blending the robust data from Kepler, K2, and TESS with modern web technology, Orbital Horizon delivers an experience that is both scientifically rigorous and engaging, inviting everyone to explore the vast universe of exoplanets.

Methodology

1. Data Preprocessing

Our process begins by harmonizing data from three key NASA missions: Kepler, K2, and TESS. We extract a uniform set of features like planet radius, orbital period, and stellar temperature and standardize the classification labels into three distinct classes: Confirmed Planet, Candidate, and False Positive. To ensure data quality, we fill in any missing values using the median of the respective column and unify feature names across all datasets. This creates a clean and consistent foundation for our machine learning models.

2. XGBClassifier Model

We use the XGBClassifier, a powerful machine learning algorithm, as our primary model. It excels at identifying complex patterns in astronomical data, making it ideal for distinguishing between real exoplanets and false alarms. We fine-tune its parameters for each dataset to achieve the highest accuracy. The model also generates feature importance plots, which show us which astronomical measurements are most critical for making a prediction, adding a layer of transparency to the AI's decisions.

3. LightGBM Model

As a complementary model, we also employ LightGBM. It is known for its incredible speed and efficiency, especially with the massive datasets common in astronomy. By using a different learning approach, LightGBM provides a valuable performance benchmark against XGBoost, ensuring our findings are robust and reliable across different analytical methods.

4. Planet Prediction

Our trained models analyze the data and calculate the probability that an object is a Confirmed Planet, a Candidate, or a False Positive. The class with the highest probability becomes the final prediction. This allows our AI to act as a triage system, flagging the most promising signals for further investigation. In the AI Workbench, users can upload their own data and get instant predictions from our specialized, pre-trained models for the Kepler, K2, and TESS datasets, ensuring the most accurate results for their specific data source.
